üéØ Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Income & Tax Setup

Start by entering your Annual Gross Income and selecting your tax filing status.

üí° Pro Tip: The order matters! Choose your Retirement Account Type first (Traditional vs Roth) as this affects everything else. Notice how filing status becomes "less critical for Roth" - that's your clue!

Step 2: Account Details

Enter your retirement account balance and expected annual return (typically 6-8% for diversified portfolios).

  • Traditional 401(k)/403(b): Withdrawals are fully taxable
  • Roth IRA: Contribution withdrawals are tax-free and penalty-free

Step 3: Market Timing (Optional)

If you're concerned about market valuations, enable the "Factor in potential market correction" feature.

  • Default: 30% correction with 3-year recovery (based on historical averages)
  • Adjust based on your market outlook and risk tolerance

Step 4: Add Your Loans

Click "+ Add Loan" for each debt you're considering paying off:

  • Purpose: Description (e.g., "Car Loan", "Credit Card")
  • Interest Rate: Annual percentage rate
  • Current Balance: Amount owed
  • Monthly Payment: Your current payment

Step 5: Configure Your Strategy

The calculator section appears once you add loans. Key decisions:

  • Withdrawal Amount: Use the slider or enable "auto-withdraw"
  • Early Penalty: Check if you're under 59¬Ω (doesn't apply to Roth)
  • Loan Allocation: Adjust how much to pay toward each loan
  • Roth Redirection: Consider redirecting freed payments to Roth IRA

Step 6: Analyze Results

The results section shows comprehensive analysis:

  • Tax Impact: Federal, state, and penalty calculations
  • Interest Savings: Total and next-12-months avoided interest
  • Foregone Growth: Opportunity cost of withdrawing funds
  • Net Impact: Bottom-line financial effect over your time horizon
