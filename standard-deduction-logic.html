üéØ What This Models

The calculator estimates the change in federal income tax from a retirement withdrawal by:

  • Computing taxable income before vs after the withdrawal
  • Applying 2024 progressive tax brackets for your selected filing status
  • Including state income tax as a flat rate applied to the full withdrawal
  • Modeling early withdrawal penalty (10%) separately if enabled
  • Accounting for deduction shielding to show how much of your withdrawal avoids federal tax

‚öôÔ∏è How Deduction Logic Works

The calculator determines which deduction to use based on your inputs:

Input Variables:

  • G = Gross income (pre-withdrawal)
  • W = Withdrawal amount
  • F = Filing status
  • S = "Apply Standard Deduction" checkbox
  • I = Itemized deduction input (if any)
  • A = Age 65+ selection

Deduction Selection Process:

If S is checked: If I > 0, use I (itemized overrides standard) Else, use D_std (standard deduction) If S is unchecked: If I > 0, use I (itemized only) Else, use 0 (treat income as already taxable)

Taxable Income Calculation:

Taxable_before = max(0, G - Deduction_used) Taxable_after = max(0, (G + W) - Deduction_used)

üõ°Ô∏è Deduction Shield Analysis

One of the calculator's most valuable features is showing how much of your withdrawal is effectively "shielded" from federal tax by unused deduction capacity:

Incremental_taxable = Taxable_after - Taxable_before Shielded_amount = max(0, W - Incremental_taxable)

Why this matters: Your effective tax rate on the withdrawal can be much lower than your marginal tax bracket when some (or all) of the withdrawal falls under unused deduction space.

üíî Early Withdrawal Penalty

  • This penalty is independent of deductions and state tax calculations
  • If you are 59¬Ω or older, leave this toggle off
  • The Age 65+ selection does NOT control the penalty - only the standard deduction

üí° UI Tips & Best Practices

  • "Apply Standard Deduction": Check this when you want the tool to compute taxable income from gross income. Uncheck if your input income is already taxable (or you want to model zero deduction)
  • Itemized Override: Enter an itemized deduction amount to override the standard deduction calculation
  • "Age 65+?": Use this only to get the additional standard deduction. It doesn't affect the early withdrawal penalty
  • Penalty Checkbox: Use this to model being under age 59¬Ω for traditional retirement accounts
