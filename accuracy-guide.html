‚ö†Ô∏è Important Limitations

State Tax Simplification

What it does: Applies a flat percentage to the full withdrawal amount.

Reality: State taxes may have their own brackets, deductions, and special rules for retirement income.

Impact: Usually minor for most states, but could be significant in states with complex retirement income rules.

Market Timing Speculation

What it does: Models user-specified correction scenarios.

Reality: Market timing is notoriously difficult; even professional investors struggle.

Impact: Use market correction features for sensitivity analysis, not as predictions.

Individual Circumstances

Missing factors: Special retirement account rules, early withdrawal exceptions, state-specific retirement income treatment, AMT implications, Medicare premium impacts, etc.

Impact: Results provide an excellent starting point but may need professional refinement.

üìã Key Assumptions

Tax Assumptions

  • Current tax law remains unchanged
  • No Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT) implications
  • Standard deduction amounts remain current
  • No special state retirement income exclusions
  • Roth withdrawals are from contributions only (not earnings)

Investment Assumptions

  • Expected returns are consistent over the time horizon
  • No account fees or transaction costs
  • Tax-deferred growth in retirement accounts
  • Market correction timing is speculative user input

Loan Assumptions

  • Fixed interest rates throughout loan terms
  • No prepayment penalties
  • Monthly payment frequency
  • No missed or late payments

üéØ Best Practices for Use

Use for Initial Analysis

The calculator excels at providing a comprehensive first-pass analysis. Use it to understand the magnitude of different factors and identify which scenarios are worth exploring further.

Compare Scenarios

The calculator's strength is in comparing different approaches (Roth vs Traditional, different withdrawal amounts, market timing scenarios). The relative differences are more reliable than absolute predictions.

Verify Key Assumptions

For significant decisions, verify key assumptions with a financial professional:

  • State tax treatment of retirement income
  • Early withdrawal penalty exceptions
  • Impact on other benefits (Social Security, Medicare)
  • Estate planning implications

ü§ù When to Consult a Professional

Consider professional advice when:

  • Large amounts: Withdrawals over $50,000
  • Complex situations: Multiple account types, state moves, early retirement
  • Tax optimization: High-income situations with complex deductions
  • Estate planning: Inheritance or beneficiary considerations
  • Benefit impacts: Potential effects on Social Security or Medicare
